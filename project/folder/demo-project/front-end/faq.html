Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1.Who can donate plasma?
    COVID recovered persons are actively encouraged to donate plasma to treat other patients.
  • 2.What are the guidelines for donating plasma?
    A plasma donor should fulfil the following criteria -Recovered people aged between 18 and 60 are eligible to donate. Plasma from pregnant women and subjects with comorbid conditions should be avoided for COVID treatment. Plasma can be donated 14 days after getting discharged.
  • 3.Are there any side effects while donating plasma?
    For COVID treatment, up to 500 ml plasma is collected from a donor's body. New plasma gets generated from the donor's bone marrow within 24-72 hours. Since the amount of plasma is less, doctors say it does not affect the donor.
  • 4.What is the compatibility of blood plasma for a particular patient?
    ABO and Rh (D) blood group compatibility: Only ABO blood group compatibility is required in plasma donation. Rh (D) blood group can be ignored, provided anti-D antibodies are not present in Rh (D) Negative donor.   • 5. What are the primary cares to be taken after plasma donation by the donor?
    The donor is kept in observation for 30 minutes after donation. Proper hydration will be ensured during observation period. Juice and water must be taken by donor. The donor is advised to take adequate fluid in next 24 hours. If any adverse effects noted in donor contact doctor immediately.
  • 6.How is the donated plasma is stored?
    The donated plasma is stored at a temperature <-30 degree Celsius. It is collected through apheresis as 200ml bags and is frozen within 8 hours. The plasma units will be transported in temperature-controlled conditions. A maximum plasma of 500 ml can be only donated.
